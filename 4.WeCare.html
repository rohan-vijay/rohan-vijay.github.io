Stanford Longevity Design Challenge

User Research, Information Architecture, Personas, UX

  1. Portfolio
  2. Stanford Longevity Design Challenge

Project Summary The aim of this project was to target a specific user group and help bridge gaps and uplift people who find themselves on the peripheries of our society – those who are perceived to be ‘different or at ‘risk’. With an active interest in trans generational products, I wanted to work and create awareness about assistive and enabling technologies in India and building the communication gap between the care-giver and the elderly specially during emergency situations.

With this aim in mind, I started with understanding the current scenario of care giving in India. After an extensive literature research, to get more views on the topic, I visited a few old age homes as well as interviewed 10 care givers in the nearby locality about their problems and view in this topic. Later followed analysis of all the insights and building a system with a wearable device and connected application for the care giver and the elderly.

The device consists of an emergency red button to send an alarm to the caregiver while in emergency situation. It has an inbuilt GPS system, photo dialer, medication alarm and speakers to aid the elderly. The connected app for the caregiver provides real time notifications of location, emergency situations, medication set and scheduling of appointments for the elderly. The project is on the concept stage with 3-d models of the device and high fidelity mockups of the application in place.
